# Introduction 
The BMW sales data from 2010 to 2024 provides a comprehensive overview of the company’s sales performance across various regions, models, and fuel types. In this analysis, we will explore the insights gained from visualizing the data and provide an overview of the key findings. 
# Insights 
- Sales Volume Distribution:
The histogram of sales volume shows a skewed distribution, indicating that most models have relatively low sales volumes, while a few models have significantly higher sales volumes.
- Top Models by Sales Volume:
The top 10 models by sales volume are dominated by popular models such as the 5 Series and i3. These models have consistently high sales volumes across different regions.
- ⁠Regional Sales Performance:
The analysis reveals that North America and Asia are the top two regions in terms of total sales volume, followed by Europe and the Middle East.
- Average Price by Model:
The average price of BMW models varies significantly, with luxury models such as the 7 Series and X5 commanding higher prices.
- Average Price by Region:
The average price of BMW models also varies by region, with North America and Europe having higher average prices compared to Asia and the Middle East.
- ⁠Fuel Type and Price:
The analysis shows that electric and hybrid models tend to have higher average prices compared to petrol and diesel models.
- ⁠Price Trend Over Time:
The average price of BMW models has increased over time, with a significant jump in recent years.
# Further Insights 
⁠- Model Popularity: 
The analysis reveals that certain models, such as the 5 Series and i3, are consistently popular across different regions. This suggests that these models have a strong brand reputation and are well-suited to the needs of customers in various markets.
- Regional Preferences: 
The data shows that different regions have different preferences when it comes to BMW models. For example, North America tends to favor larger, more luxurious models, while Asia prefers more compact and fuel-efficient models.
- Fuel Type Trends:
The analysis indicates that there is a growing trend towards electric and hybrid vehicles, particularly in regions with strong environmental regulations. This suggests that BMW is well-positioned to capitalize on the growing demand for eco-friendly vehicles.
- Pricing Strategy:
The data suggests that BMW has a premium pricing strategy, with higher prices for luxury models and lower prices for more entry-level models. This strategy is likely driven by the company's brand reputation and the perceived value of its products.
- ⁠Sales Volume and Price Correlation:
The analysis reveals a positive correlation between sales volume and price for certain models. This suggests that higher-priced models tend to have lower sales volumes, while lower-priced models tend to have higher sales volumes.
- ⁠Year-over-Year Sales Trends:
The data shows that BMW's sales have been steadily increasing over the years, with some fluctuations. This suggests that the company has been successful in maintaining a strong sales performance despite changes in market conditions.
- ⁠Regional Sales Growth:
The analysis indicates that certain regions, such as Asia, have experienced significant sales growth over the years. This suggests that BMW has been successful in expanding its presence in these markets.
